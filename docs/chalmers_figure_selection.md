## Why These Three

### 1. EU Scenario Decomposition

Use this first because it communicates the core scientific question fast:

- compare conservation, material use, and bioenergy,
- show the climate result at EU scale,
- explain *why* each scenario performs the way it does.

This is the clearest figure for a hiring committee member who wants to understand the main research logic in under thirty seconds.

### 2. Constrained NUTS-2 Policy Map

Use this second because it shows:

- real spatial analysis,
- subnational European relevance,
- explicit forest-versus-food trade-offs,
- a more policy-facing result than a simple country ranking.

This is the figure that most strongly signals direct overlap with the advertisement’s emphasis on European and sub-national land-use trade-offs.

### 3. Constrained Portfolio Summary

Use this third because it proves that the project is not only descriptive. It shows:

- the selected policy mix,
- the biomass-supply safeguard,
- the food-capacity safeguard,
- the fact that the final result comes from a constrained allocation problem.

This is the figure that helps the committee see the “biophysical and economic models” angle.

## Backup Figures

If you are allowed a fourth figure, add:

- [`eu_uncertainty_ranges.png`](../figures/eu_uncertainty_ranges.png)

Why:

- it shows methodological maturity,
- it makes clear that the project tests robustness rather than only reporting one deterministic answer.

If you are allowed a fifth figure, add:

- [`nuts2_forest_food_tradeoff.png`](../figures/nuts2_forest_food_tradeoff.png)

Why:

- it makes the forest-versus-food screening trade-off more intuitive,
- it complements the map with a clearer comparison view.

If you want one Sweden-specific figure for conversation rather than submission, use:

- [`sweden_nuts2_empirical_comparison.png`](../figures/sweden_nuts2_empirical_comparison.png)

Why:

- it shows that you improved the weakest regional layer with empirical forestry data,
- it is a good oral discussion figure if they ask how you handled subnational evidence and model revision.

## One-Sentence Caption Set

Use these if you need very short application captions:

1. `EU scenario decomposition`: "Compares the climate effects of conservation, material cascade, and bioenergy pathways and shows why the scenarios differ."
2. `Constrained NUTS-2 policy map`: "Shows how forest and food-land priorities vary across European regions under explicit biomass-supply and food-capacity safeguards."
3. `Optimized portfolio summary`: "Summarizes the selected regional policy mix and demonstrates that the project solves a constrained allocation problem rather than only ranking regions independently."

## Suggested Narrative Order

If you present the three recommended figures in a brief:

1. Start with the EU decomposition figure to establish the carbon-accounting logic and main result.
2. Move to the constrained NUTS-2 policy map to show spatial heterogeneity and land competition.
3. End with the constrained portfolio summary to show that the project moves from comparison to explicit policy design.
