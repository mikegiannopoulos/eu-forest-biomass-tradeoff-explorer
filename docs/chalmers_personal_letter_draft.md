# Personal Letter Draft

Dear Members of the Recruitment Committee,

I am applying for the doctoral position in Land Use and Climate Impacts at the Division of Physical Resource Theory. I am drawn to this position because it sits exactly at the intersection of the questions I want to work on: how land-use systems, biomass use, carbon accounting, and policy design can be analyzed together in a way that is quantitatively rigorous and still relevant for real decision-making.

What especially attracts me to this position is the explicit focus on the tension between leaving trees standing to store carbon and harvesting biomass to substitute for fossil-intensive materials and energy. I also find the broader framing of trade-offs between wood, food, energy, and conservation highly compelling. That combination of systems analysis, climate relevance, and policy-facing modeling is the kind of work I want to develop further at doctoral level.

To make my interest concrete, I built a portfolio project directly aligned with this research area: `eu-forest-biomass-tradeoff-explorer`. In that project, I combined FAOSTAT land-use and forestry data with Eurostat GISCO and Eurostat land-cover and crop-production data to analyze EU forest biomass allocation scenarios and subnational land-use trade-offs. The project compares conservation-oriented harvest reduction, material-cascade use of wood, and bioenergy expansion, and then extends the analysis to NUTS-2 regions where forestry choices are compared with a food-land safeguard. In its latest version, the project also includes a constrained regional allocation model that selects a Europe-wide policy mix while maintaining biomass supply and protecting food-related land capacity. I developed it deliberately as a transparent screening and decision-support model, not as an overclaimed replica of larger frameworks such as TERRAMARE, ClimAg, or CRAFT.

This work strengthened my interest in exactly the kinds of questions highlighted in the position. It required me to define system boundaries, work with carbon-accounting logic, handle spatial data, compare scenarios, test sensitivity to assumptions, and think carefully about what can and cannot be claimed from a simplified model. Just as importantly, it made me want to deepen the methodological side further: stronger biophysical and economic modeling, better treatment of agriculture, richer subnational data, and closer integration of policy constraints and environmental trade-offs.

I believe I would be a strong fit for this doctoral position because I am motivated by interdisciplinary environmental systems analysis, comfortable with quantitative modeling and scientific computing in Python, and strongly interested in building the deeper domain expertise needed for research on land use and climate impacts. I would be excited to contribute to the Division of Physical Resource Theory’s work on sustainability transitions and to develop, within that environment, the next generation of tools and analyses for land-use and biomass policy questions.

Thank you for your consideration. I would be very glad to discuss my background and motivation further.

## Safer Positioning Notes

Use this project to signal:

- strong fit with the advertised research theme,
- readiness for quantitative and spatial modeling work,
- genuine motivation for forestry-food-energy-conservation trade-off analysis,
- awareness of model limitations and research next steps.

- that the repository is equivalent to TERRAMARE, ClimAg, or CRAFT,
